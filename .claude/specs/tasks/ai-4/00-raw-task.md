Task 3: Blog Module — SEO Machine

Description:
Architecture: Blog built on SSR/SSG integrated into the existing React/Vite stack — every article page must be server-side rendered so Google bots see the full content without executing JavaScript. No lazy-loading of article content.

Structure of each post: Every blog entry must include: a slug (SEO-friendly URL, e.g. /blog/how-ai-shortens-development), a meta-title optimized for target keywords, a meta-description up to 160 characters that encourages clicks, and an og:image (a dedicated graphic for each article — ensures a clean preview when shared on LinkedIn and other platforms).

Blog module on the homepage: A section on the homepage displaying the 3 most recent articles — cards with a title, short description, and a "Read more" button.

Three first posts in polish to write (minimum 1,000 characters):
"Jak AI skraca development o 70%?" — keywords: workflow automation, AI-assisted coding, faster MVP. Goal: showcase delivery speed and target clients looking to save time.
"Dlaczego software w 2026 jest tańszy niż myślisz?" — keywords: AI application cost, IT cost optimization, affordable software. Goal: attract clients with smaller budgets and debunk the myth that good software has to be expensive.
"Od pomysłu do SaaS w tydzień — to możliwe?" — keywords: LLM integration, Prompt Engineering, MVP in a week. Goal: direct sales of the Rapid Prototyping service.

SEO: Every article's content saturated with high-intent phrases: "AI application", "AI programming", "AI software house". H1–H3 headings containing these phrases. JSON-LD (ProfessionalService) implementation at the site level — tells Google bots what you do and builds authority in the AI development niche. Content cluster: every article internally links to the offer and other posts, creating a network of connections that drives conversion.
